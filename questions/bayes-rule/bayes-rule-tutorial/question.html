Bayes' Theorem:
$$ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)\cdot P(A)}{P(B)} $$
Remember:
$ A,B $ = events
$ P(A|B) $ = probability of A given B is true
$ P(B|A) $ = probability of B given A is true
$ P(A), P(B) $ = the independent probabilities of A and B

Two factories A and B make the same gadget. D is the probability that the gadget is defective. {{params.pa}}% of the gadgets come from factory A and {{params.pb}}% of the gadgets come from factory B. {{params.pda}}% of the gadgets from A are defective, and {{params.pdb}}% from B are defective.
Given the gadget is defective, what is the probability it is from factory A?

$$ P(A) = {{params.pa_decimal}} $$ $$ P(B) = \text{ ?} $$

$$ P(A\cap B) = P(D|A)P(A) = {{params.pda_decimal}} \cdot {{params.pa_decimal}} = {{params.pand}} $$ $$ P(B\cap D) = P(D|B)P(B) = {{params.pdb_decimal}} \cdot {{params.pb_decimal}} = \text{ ?} $$

$$ P(D) = P(A\cap D) + P(B\cap D) = \text{ ?} $$

And the final answer is: $$ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)\cdot P(A)}{P(B)} = \text{ ?} $$
